Description

In response to the cyber-attack on Bangladesh Bank in February 2016, the Government of Bangladesh's Computer Incident Response Team, known as BGD e-GOV CIRT, was established in March 2016 under Section 6 of the BCC Act 1990, as a proactive initiative by the Information and Communication Technology Division.
Also, Section 9 of the Cyber Security Act, 2023 directs the formation of a National Computer Emergency Response Team (N-CERT) under the National Cyber Security Agency. BGD e-GOV CIRT has been given additional responsibility of National Computer Emergency Response Team as per Office Order of Information and Communication Technology Department.
National Data Center and network connectivity between all government offices across the country has been setup to deliver e-services at citizen's doorsteps. National Data Centers and online services of financial institutions are being hit by various cyber attacks. BGD e-GOV CIRT is conducting necessary activities under BCC to protect Critical Information Infrastructure (CII) from all these attacks.
BGD e-GOV CIRT is responsible for maintaining cooperation and communication with international organizations, forums and CERT/CSIRT/CIRT of other countries engaged in cyber security for the purpose of securing the cyber space of Bangladesh.
Bangladesh is currently ranked 36th in the National Cybersecurity Index and recognized as a Tier-1 Role Model country in the Global Cybersecurity Index (GCI) 2024, published by the International Telecommunication Union (ITU).
